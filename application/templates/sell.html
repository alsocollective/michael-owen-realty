CASE STUDY:
Moving From Condo to House

client needs

  • Selling a condo and purchasing a home.
  • Looking for more space, more bedrooms, including a backyard wtih parking.
  • Working with a limited budget & need the highest selling price for their condo.

the selling process

Considering the market conditions, Michael advised his clients to purchase a new home prior to selling their condominium. Michael also suggested his clients, who were quite handy, begin preparing their condo for sale while searching for a new house. We found a home downtown Toronto with an open building permit and a half completed renovation. This scared most other buyers off and we saw an opportunity to get a solid home for a fair price in a super hot seller's market. This type of purchase does come with some risk but after a full assessment we determined it was manageable considering the clients also wanted to complete a few other renovations.

The property was closed quickly so the work could begin simultaneously as we were finishing the preparations for selling the condominium and getting it on the market. We held back on offers and priced the condo just above the current market value and received one offer on the property.

outcomes

Michael's clients were able to complete the majority of the renovations on their new home prior to moving in. We agreed to a longer closing on the condominium allowing a few weeks to complete some of the renovations at the new home.

CASE STUDY:
Selling Reconsidered

client needs

  • Selling a home as part of an Estate.
  • Located downtown Toronto and not looking to do any repairs.
  • Looking for a quick sale for the highest selling price possible.

the selling process

Considering the market conditions Michael advised his clients to prepare the home and remove, donate or sell the unwanted personal belongings in the home. A family should not be rushed though this process and Michael suggested these activities to ultimately help the sale of the property move more smoothly. The home was in need of some repairs and some cosmetic upgrades but rather the renovating the home we decided to hold back offers until a particular date and we set the asking price just below the current market value and in relation to the required repairs. We received 5 offers in total and the home sold for the maximum amount the market would allow for a home in less then perfect condition.

outcomes

Through the steps Michael outlined, the clients benefited from a smooth transaction and with 5 offers on the property we were able to maximum the market potential and the clients received the highest price possible for the property.

Preparation
is key

Preparing the property is an obvious task in marketing and selling real estate, the questions is, what do you do and where do you need to spend money? If any. This all comes down to the strategy you are employing and of course the current market conditions. Assessing your property's advantages and disadvantages, assembling the required documents and information, developing a strategy and determining a marketing price is all part of the preparations and is arguable the most import step.

Typical Documents

There are many documents required to sell a property plus other documentation that is not required but can be very helpful in achieving your goals and further protecting your interests.

Cost
of Selling

The costs of selling a property are typically covered by the funds the seller receives from the buyer in the purchase of the property. The agent you choose and the strategy you employ can effect the net amount the seller receives from the sale of the property. Depending on the strategy, your costs may include renovations to the property, a survey, home inspection fees, legal fees and moving expenses.

Determining
the Value

Ultimately the market determines the true value of a property. The property characteristics need to be taken into consideration before you are able to compare the property to other similar properties and establish a competitive price. There are other factors that can affect the selling price of your property such as the market conditions, property characteristics and having the right Real Estate sales representative.

Avoiding
Costly
Mistakes

Engaging an experienced real estate agent you can trust will ensure your interests are protected and you avoid costly pitfalls. Overpricing the property and employing the wrong strategy in relation to the market conditions is most common mistake people make with selling a property.

Competitive Advantages

There are many competitive advantages to each property, leveraging those advantages in context with the current market conditions is crucial in a successful strategy.
